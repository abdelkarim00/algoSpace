Centripetal Forces in Newton's Principia

Definition V: Centripetal Force

"Vis centripeta est qua corpora versus punctum aliquod veluti ad centrum undique trahuntur, impelluntur, vel utcunque tendunt."

Translation: "A centripetal force is that by which bodies are drawn, impelled, or tend toward a point as to a center."

Key Characteristics

  • Always directed perpendicular to motion (toward center)
  • Changes direction of velocity without changing magnitude
  • Not a fundamental force but a description of effect
  • Modern formula: F = mv²/r

Key Propositions

Proposition I: Area Law

"Areas, quas corpora in gyros acta radiis ad centrum virium ductis describunt, esse in planis immobilibus & temporibus proportionales."

Translation: "The areas which revolving bodies describe by radii drawn to a center of force lie in fixed planes and are proportional to the times."

dA/dt = constant

Significance:

  • Proves Kepler's Second Law mathematically
  • Implies conservation of angular momentum
  • Planets move faster when closer to the Sun

Proposition IV: Force in Circular Motion

Established the fundamental relationship:

F ∝ v²/r

Newton's Geometric Proof:

  1. Considered infinitesimal displacement triangles
  2. Showed deflection ∝ (arc length)²/radius
  3. Since force ∝ deflection/time², concluded F ∝ v²/r

Proposition XI: Elliptical Orbits

"Si corpus revolvatur in ellipsi: requiritur lex vis centripetæ tendentis ad umbilicum ellipsis."

Translation: "If a body revolves in an ellipse; it is required to find the law of the centripetal force tending to the focus of the ellipse."

F ∝ 1/r²

Breakthrough:

  • First mathematical proof that elliptical orbits require inverse-square law forces
  • Unified celestial and terrestrial physics
  • Foundation for law of universal gravitation

Applications in Principia

Application Principia Reference Significance
Planetary Orbits Book III, Propositions VIII-XV Derived Kepler's laws from centripetal gravity
Moon's Motion Book III, Proposition IV Showed lunar orbit governed by same force as falling objects
Tides Book III, Proposition XXIV Explained via lunar/solar centripetal forces
Conical Pendulum Book I, Proposition XXIV Analyzed circular motion under tension and gravity

Newton's Proof Techniques

  1. Geometric Limits: Used "method of first and last ratios" (proto-calculus)
  2. Infinitesimal Analysis: Compared vanishingly small triangles
  3. Curvature Measurement: Related force to versed sine (sagitta) of arcs
  4. Dynamical Synthesis: Combined inertia with continuous deflection

Modern Interpretation

F = mω²r = mv²/r

Where ω = angular velocity (rad/s)

Legacy:

  • Foundation for central force problems in classical mechanics
  • Precursor to concepts in general relativity
  • Essential for understanding rotational dynamics
  • Basis for centrifugal/Coriolis effects in rotating frames
