Motion in Conic Sections in Newton's Principia

Fundamental Theorem

Newton proved that inverse-square centripetal forces necessarily produce motion along conic sections:

F ∝ 1/r² ⇒ Conic Section Orbit

where the force center lies at one focus of the conic.

Proposition XI: Elliptical Orbits

"Si corpus revolvatur in ellipsi: requiritur lex vis centripetæ tendentis ad umbilicum ellipsis."

Translation: "If a body revolves in an ellipse; it is required to find the law of the centripetal force tending to the focus of the ellipse."

F = GMm/r²

Newton's Geometric Proof:

  1. Used properties of ellipses (latus rectum = 2b²/a)
  2. Showed angular momentum h = r²θ' remains constant
  3. Related force to curvature via "method of first and last ratios"
  4. Proved only inverse-square law produces stable ellipses

Proposition VI: General Force Law

For any curved path, Newton derived:

F ∝ (chord length)² / (sagitta × time²)

Where sagitta is the "arrow" height of the orbital arc.

Legacy

  • Kepler's Laws: Mathematically proved all three laws
  • Universal Gravitation: Unified celestial and terrestrial physics
  • Spaceflight: Basis for Hohmann transfer orbits
  • Modern Physics: Template for other inverse-square laws (Coulomb force)

Why This Matters

Newton's analysis showed for the first time that:

  1. The same physical laws govern both earthly and celestial motions
  2. Orbital shapes reveal the underlying force law
  3. Mathematics could reliably predict cosmic phenomena
